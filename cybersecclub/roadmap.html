Guide to CTF Prep/Becoming a 1337 Hacker

Prerequisites and General Advice

I strongly recommend general programming knowledge. This course is a good starting point for complete beginners to programming and there are a ton more online.
If you know C/C++ and/or Assembly-bonus points. You will like reverse engineering and binary exploitation.
If you know JavaScript, PHP, and/or SQL, you will enjoy web security.
I chose tutorials that were hands-on and engaging so readers don't lose interest. CTF stands for Capture the Flag, which are hacking contests that happen online every weekend:
A rule of thumb is to try each problem for 20 minutes to the best of your ability-use google, look at man pages and online documentation THEN ask for help.
Most websites like HacktheBox, TryHackMe, picoCTF, and others have Discords that are full of professionals.
Another site you may want to checkout is meetup.com, to meet other hackers and coders who can mentor you, or your local 2600 hacker group(i.e. Phoenix).
